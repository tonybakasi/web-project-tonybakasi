Christ's Mission

Jesus was sent into the world so that people may enjoy life in God's presence. According to 14:6, the purpose of his sending is for people to "come" to the Father, which in the immediate context implies to know and believe in God. After defining himself as the conduit via which people communicate, "When Jesus adds, "If you know me, you will know my Father as well" (erchesthai, 14:6), he turns to the word "know." From now on you do know him and have seen him" (14:7). "Whoever has seen me has seen the Father.. Do you not believe that I am in the Father and the Father is in me?" he asks, shifting from "knowing" to "believing." (14:9). In this verse, as elsewhere in the Gospel, coming, knowing, and believing are overlapping terms for human connections with God (e.g., 1:10-12, 6:35, 68-69, 7:37-38).

Jesus' goal was to find and rescue those who were lost. This redemption and good news was and continues to be geared at every need, poverty, and human dilemma. Man is, by nature, estranged from God. He is plagued by a slew of troubles and calamities, and he desperately needs to hear the good news of Christ's love, grace, and favour. Because God had anointed him with the Holy Spirit, Jesus was certain that he would be able to complete his mission.
